Elk Habitat Prediction System

Using Machine Learning to Predict Optimal Elk Hunting Habitats in Colorado

Explore Interactive Map

Project Overview

The Elk Habitat Prediction System is a sophisticated machine learning-based approach to identifying optimal elk hunting habitats in Colorado. By analyzing terrain, vegetation, and other environmental factors, the system classifies areas by hunting suitability (high, medium, low) with remarkable accuracy.

This project integrates multiple geospatial datasets including digital elevation models, satellite imagery, and historical harvest records to create a comprehensive prediction model that achieved 97.1% accuracy in classifying habitat suitability.

Our research focuses on Colorado Game Management Units (GMUs) 12, 23, and 24, which are known for their significant elk populations and diverse terrain. The system provides actionable intelligence for both wildlife managers and hunters, offering data-driven guidance for conservation planning and hunting strategies.

Key Features

Comprehensive Data Integration

Our system combines multiple geospatial datasets to create a holistic view of the landscape:

  • Digital Elevation Models (USGS)
  • Sentinel-2 satellite imagery
  • Colorado Parks & Wildlife elk harvest statistics
  • OpenStreetMap road/trail networks
  • National Hydrography Dataset water features
  • Land ownership boundaries

Advanced Machine Learning

Our Random Forest Classifier model:

  • Achieves 97.1% prediction accuracy
  • Processes 19 environmental features
  • Classifies areas into high, medium, and low suitability
  • Provides probability scores for each classification
  • Quantifies the importance of each environmental factor
  • Handles complex, non-linear relationships between variables

Practical Applications

The system provides valuable tools for multiple stakeholders:

  • Hunters: Identify promising locations based on data-driven predictions
  • Wildlife Managers: Inform conservation priorities and land use planning
  • Researchers: Understand elk habitat preferences quantitatively
  • Land Use Planners: Consider wildlife habitat in development decisions
  • Conservation Organizations: Target protection efforts effectively

Key Findings

Our analysis revealed that terrain characteristics, particularly slope (40.8% importance), play the dominant role in determining elk habitat suitability. Elk typically prefer moderate slopes (10-30Â°) that balance accessibility with security from predators.

Vegetation metrics, including NDVI (13.7%) and NDMI (4.4%), collectively formed the second most influential category of predictors, highlighting the importance of both forage availability and cover.

Elevation (12.3%) emerged as the third most significant individual feature, reflecting elk's preference for specific elevation ranges that offer optimal environmental conditions.

The significance of edge proximity (5.8%) confirms the importance of forest-meadow interfaces, where elk can quickly transition between feeding and cover areas.
